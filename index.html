About us

Hi! I’m passionate about creating a cleaner, more sustainable future through renewable energy. Important data about the state of energy production worlwide

Global renewable energy capacity increased by 9.1% in 2022, reaching a total capacity of 3,378 gigawatts (GW), with solar and wind leading the expansion.

In 2022, solar power accounted for 60% of new renewable energy capacity additions, continuing to grow as the leading technology in renewable energy production.

Renewables provided nearly 30% of global electricity generation in 2022, with hydropower, wind, and solar contributing the most.

Why is Non-Renewable Energy bad for our future?

Non-renewable energy sources, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, are harmful because they release large amounts of greenhouse gases when burned, contributing to climate change and air pollution. These fuels are also limited resources, meaning they will eventually run out, leading to higher costs and energy insecurity. In addition, extracting and transporting fossil fuels often damages ecosystems and endangers wildlife. Relying on non-renewable energy not only harms the environment but also delays progress toward a cleaner, more sustainable future.
